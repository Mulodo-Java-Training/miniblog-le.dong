Following our Capital One Cup final win over Tottenham, Chelsea fans are dreaming of a little bit of history repeated.

Posted by le.dong on August 24, 2014

Edit Delete

It was, after all, 10 years ago that Jose Mourinho lifted his first piece of silverware with Chelsea. That too came after a hard-fought League Cup final win and back then it provided a platform for even greater success to follow in the months ahead. So there's reason for encouragement. On that afternoon in 2005, the Blues battled to an extra-time win over Liverpool, thanks in no small part to goals from Didier Drogba and Mateja Kezman. Captain John Terry was heroic at the back in Cardiff and as fate would have it, he was at his colossal best this time around too. Back then, Chelsea lifted the trophy before getting straight back down to business. There are three crucial months left this season yet and Mourinho and Chelsea are not the type to rest on their laurels. The significance of the win shouldn't be underestimated. Sure, it's not the trophy that every club dreams about winning at the start of the season - but it is a symbol of achievement. It is a confidence boost. It's proof that this Chelsea squad have the ability to win silverware. And that will stand us in good stead for the remaining months of this season. There are two competitions remaining for Chelsea. We command a five-point lead and a game in hand over Manchester City in the Premier League title race. There are 12 games to go, starting with a difficult trip across London to face West Ham on Wednesday night. The small matter of Paris Saint-Germain's visit to Stamford Bridge in the Champions League last 16 second leg follows next week, but as is the message from the players, Chelsea need to take each game as it comes. West Ham are no pushovers and have been enjoying an improved season under Sam Allardyce. On Boxing Day in the reverse fixture, the Blues managed to come out on top with a 2-0 win. We racked up 28 shots on goal and deserved the three points, but West Ham's recent below-par form is something they'll be keen to address. Having not won in their last five matches and currently hurt by defeat to Crystal Palace at the weekend, Allardyce will be challenging his players to prove a point. But Chelsea will be raring to go too. We can take a huge amount of confidence from our first trophy win since Mourinho returned in 2013 and the success of one decade ago shows just how much of a shot in the arm the win over Spurs could prove to be. Chelsea remained unbeaten domestically for the final 11 games after lifting the League Cup in 2005 -winning eight of them - and finished 12 points clear of the chasing pack come the end of the season. It was our first league win in 50 years. It's up to the players to take the confidence from the weekend's win and translate it into other fixtures, just like we've done in the past. The match at Upton Park is a challenge, but it's a great opportunity to build on our win for the battles ahead. The League Cup win - as well as being a great achievement - is a stepping stone to further glories. It's down to Chelsea now to ensure that momentum continues. By Scott Saunders, Chelsea fan.
